Distilled has been created to offer a different approach to information management from a "news & information" perspective.

One of the primary issues is that complex topics are not translated - or, distilled - for general consumption. New channels themselves are not good at understanding topics or translating them, and the same can also be said about the pundits which are asked to speak.

Distilled will offer a unique approach to acquiring information and knowledge about many topics. First, a simple and common headline about an event is given. These are listed on the left-hand sidebar. You will notice that there are three different colours that border these. The first one, red, is something that would be considered important or a priority - you should probably read these! Next, are the orange topics. These are topics that would be good to 'observe' - to have an idea that they are occurring and to think about, but that might not necessarily require your immediate attention or thought. Finally, the green-outlined headlines are 'upcoming' topics. These are topics which should also be observed, but whose end results or consequences may be further down the horizon. When you hover over a headline, a popup will appear which gives you the first distillation of that information - this will still be in relatively simple terms with the goal that anyone can understand, regarldess the topic being discussed. From here, you can dive further into a full analysis from Distilled and also find the article from which the original source has come. We will always prioritize a direct source (scientific article, etc).
